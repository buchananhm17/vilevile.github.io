Sadly throughout this year, progress in every area of sustainable energy falls short of the targets needed to be met for renewable energy and energy efficiency. We will need more countries to embrace new technology on a much wider scale. Globally, 85.3 per cent of the population had access to electricity in 2014, an increase of only 0.3 percentage points since 2012. That means that 1.06 billion people, predominantly rural dwellers, still function without electricity. Half of those people live in sub-Saharan Africa.
